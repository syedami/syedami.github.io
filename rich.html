Rich Hall

Situated in the vibrant West Campus of Boston University, Rich Hall offers contemporary amenities and a comfortable living experience for students looking for a well-rounded on-campus life. Explore the features that make Rich Hall an attractive choice for those who value modern comfort and convenience.

Room Options

Here are the various room options, to find the ideal setup for your needs.

Single Room

1. Private Bathroom: Single rooms in Rich Hall may offer private or semi-private bathrooms, providing exclusive access to bathroom facilities.
2. Kitchenette: Some single rooms may include a small kitchenette with a microwave, mini-fridge, and sink, allowing residents to prepare light meals and snacks in the comfort of their room.
3.Personal Study Space: In addition to a desk and chair, single rooms may have a dedicated study nook or workspace to provide residents with an ideal environment for focused academics.
4. Enhanced Internet Access: Single rooms may come with enhanced or upgraded internet connectivity, ensuring a seamless online experience for academic and personal needs.
5. Balcony or View: Depending on the room's location, some single rooms may offer balconies or scenic views, providing residents with a unique living experience.

Double Room

1. High-Speed Internet Access: Residents can enjoy reliable and high-speed internet connectivity, ensuring seamless online activities for academic and personal use.
2. Individual Study Desks: Each resident has access to their own study desk and chair, providing a dedicated space for academic work and study.
3. Shared Bathroom Facilities: Double rooms typically have access to shared bathroom facilities, which may include showers, sinks, and toilets located nearby on the same floor.
4. Closets or Wardrobes: Ample storage space is provided with closets or wardrobes for each resident, ensuring that clothing and personal items can be stored efficiently.
5. Community Lounges: Rich Hall often features common lounges and shared spaces, providing opportunities for residents to socialize, relax, and engage with fellow students

Triple Room

1. Multiple Beds: Triple rooms typically come with multiple beds, arranged as bunk beds or lofted beds, providing individual sleeping areas for each resident.
2. Study Areas: Triple rooms are equipped with multiple desks and chairs, offering dedicated study areas for each resident, ensuring they have their personal workspace.
3. Shared Storage: To accommodate the needs of all residents, triple rooms usually provide shared storage solutions such as wardrobes or closets, allowing everyone to store their clothing and personal belongings.

Quad Room

1. Bed and Mattress: The room will usually be equipped with four single beds, each furnished with a comfortable mattress to cater to all four residents.
2. Desk and Chair: Four separate desks and chairs will be provided, ensuring ample workspace for individual study needs.
3. Private Bathroom: Some quad rooms may offer a private or semi-private bathroom, providing exclusive access to bathroom facilities for the residents in the room. 4. Kitchenette: Quad rooms might include a shared kitchenette with a microwave, mini-fridge, and sink, allowing roommates to prepare light meals and snacks. 5. Dining Area: In addition to desks and chairs, some quad rooms may feature a small dining area, providing roommates with space for shared meals and socializing.
